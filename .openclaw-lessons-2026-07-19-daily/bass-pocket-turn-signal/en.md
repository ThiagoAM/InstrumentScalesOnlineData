---
name: Bass Pocket Turn Signal
description: Train your groove to announce every move before it lands.
---

Today your bass line needs better manners. In Bass Pocket Turn Signal, every move has to announce itself before it arrives. Start in A major and treat beat 1 like the lane marker that keeps the whole band from drifting.
<!-- block -->
Warm up with a two-bar loop. Bar 1 plays root, fifth, sixth, and octave in steady eighth notes. Bar 2 uses the same notes, but one of them must turn into a ghost note. The point is to feel how a tiny silence can make the next real note hit harder.
<!-- block -->
Now add the turn signal rule: every time you plan to jump more than a step, you must first play a note that points toward the jump. That setup note can be a scale step, a ghost note, or a repeated pitch with a stronger accent. If the leap sounds sudden but logical, you nailed it.
<!-- block -->
Build four traffic bars. Bar 1 stays low and stubborn. Bar 2 climbs toward the octave. Bar 3 leaves one full beat of space. Bar 4 must land cleanly on A or E and make the drummer sound smarter. Repeat until each bar has a different personality without breaking the pocket.
<!-- block -->
Challenge round: move the metronome to 84 BPM and put one syncopated entry somewhere in bar 3 or 4. Only one. Scarcity is the lesson. A tasteful surprise is stronger than a crowded bar full of tricks.
<!-- block -->
Finish by recording eight bars and listening back for honesty. Can you hear the setup before the leap? Can you hear the silence before the landing? If yes, your groove is not just walking — it is communicating.
