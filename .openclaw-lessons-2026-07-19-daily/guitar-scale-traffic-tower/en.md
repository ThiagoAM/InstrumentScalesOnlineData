---
name: Guitar Scale Traffic Tower
description: Direct your scale movement with lane changes, accents, and smart landings.
---

Welcome to the Guitar Scale Traffic Tower. Today you are the controller, not the passenger. Pick D major in one position and imagine every note as a car that needs a clean lane, a clear signal, and a safe landing.
<!-- block -->
Start with a control check. Play the scale ascending and descending in eighth notes at 72 BPM. Accent only beats 1 and 3, then repeat while saying the scale degrees out loud. If a note buzzes or the pick angle feels messy, that lane is congested.
<!-- block -->
Now add turn signals. Before every string crossing, slightly pause your picking hand in the air for a split second, then cross with intention instead of luck. Your mission is to make every crossing feel predicted, not rescued at the last second.
<!-- block -->
Build a traffic pattern: bar 1 climbs straight up the scale, bar 2 descends, bar 3 skips one note every time you change strings, and bar 4 must resolve to the nearest D on beat 1. Loop all four bars until the route feels smooth and your tone stays even.
<!-- block -->
Challenge round: keep the same pattern, but move the accents to beats 2 and 4 and add one slide into a target note during bar 4. You are training two skills at once here—time feel and fretboard vision—so do not chase speed if the map gets blurry.
<!-- block -->
Finish with a short solo test. Improvise four bars using only notes from the same D major position, but every phrase must end on either the root or the fifth. If the line sounds organized and alive, your traffic tower is working.
