---
name: Piano Scale Wind Tunnel
description: Test your scale control under pressure and keep every note stable.
---

Welcome to the Piano Scale Wind Tunnel. Imagine that every uneven finger, rushing rhythm, or collapsing wrist is a gust of wind trying to knock your scale off course. Today you will practice keeping the line stable while the pressure increases.
<!-- block -->
Choose B-flat major or another key that already makes you slightly suspicious. Play one octave hands separately, then two octaves hands together, at a calm tempo. Your only job is to keep the tone level from the first note to the last, as if the air around the keyboard were perfectly smooth.
<!-- block -->
Now turn on the wind. Add a crescendo going up and a decrescendo coming down, but do not let the tempo speed up with the volume. If the louder notes make your shoulders tense or your thumbs crash, the tunnel is winning and you need a smaller dynamic change.
<!-- block -->
Next, place a checkpoint at every thumb crossing. Stop for one beat right before the crossing, then continue without losing the pulse. This tiny pause teaches your hands to prepare instead of panic, which is exactly what clean scale playing needs under pressure.
<!-- block -->
Challenge round: keep the same scale, but switch articulation every two beats — two beats legato, two beats light staccato, two beats legato again. The sound should change, but the hand shape and rhythmic spine should stay trustworthy.
<!-- block -->
Finish with a pilot test. Play the full scale once more and ask three questions: did the tone stay centered, did the rhythm stay calm, and did the crossings stay organized? If two answers are yes, the tunnel is helping. If all three are yes, you are flying.
