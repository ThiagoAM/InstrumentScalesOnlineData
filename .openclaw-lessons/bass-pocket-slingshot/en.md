---
name: Pocket Slingshot in D Minor
description: Launch tight bass grooves by pulling away from the root, storing tension, and snapping back into the pocket.
---
This lesson turns your bass line into a slingshot. Work in **D natural minor** over **Dm - Bb - F - C** at **76-90 BPM**. Your job is to pull the groove away from home just enough to create tension, then snap it back without wrecking the time.
<!-- block -->
Set three slingshot rules:
- beat **1** must state a clear chord tone
- one note per bar must move **away from D** by step
- one note per bar must **snap back** by leap or strong landing
- one moment per bar must be real silence

Loop two bars until the pulse feels heavy and calm.
<!-- block -->
Round 1 is the **stretch round**. Start from D, F, or A, then travel one or two scale steps farther than feels comfortable before returning. If the line sounds nervous instead of confident, your stretch is too random.
<!-- block -->
Round 2 is the **release round**. After the stretch, make the comeback note feel inevitable by using one of these tools:
- a longer note value
- a stronger accent
- a cleaner, emptier space before landing

The return should feel like gravity, not luck.
<!-- block -->
Now record **45 seconds** over the full progression. Keep the groove simple enough to repeat, but change where the stretch happens in each pass. You are training control: same pocket, new tension point.
<!-- block -->
Check your take:
- Did the root feel stronger after each detour?
- Did the silence help the return hit harder?
- Which comeback note made the whole groove breathe?

Final upgrade: move the slingshot game to **G minor** and keep the same groove logic. If the pocket survives the key change, the slingshot works. 🎸
