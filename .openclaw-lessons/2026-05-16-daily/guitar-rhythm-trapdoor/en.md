---
name: Guitar Rhythm Trapdoor
description: Use surprise rests and offbeat landings to make scale phrases snap harder without losing the groove.
---
Choose **E minor pentatonic** or **A Dorian** in one position and set a metronome or backing track to **84-98 BPM**. Imagine the floor under your solo has hidden trapdoors: every time you leave the obvious beat, you either land with style or fall through the phrase. Your job is to make the surprises feel intentional, musical, and a little dangerous.
<!-- block -->
Round 1 is the **safe step round**. Play a two-bar phrase where beat 1 is solid, but one note in bar 2 lands on an offbeat. Keep the note choice simple and focus on the timing. If the phrase sounds random, simplify the rhythm before changing any notes.
<!-- block -->
Round 2 is the **trapdoor round**. Build four short phrases and give each one a different surprise:
- leave beat 2 empty
- start on the **and** of 1
- hold one note longer than expected
- answer a busy phrase with a tiny two-note reply

The notes can stay inside the scale, but the rhythm has to create the drama.
<!-- block -->
Round 3 is the **rescue round**. Deliberately play a phrase that feels slightly late or crowded, then fix it on the next bar by landing clearly on **E, G, or A**. This trains you to recover musically instead of freezing when a phrase gets messy.
<!-- block -->
Final challenge: record **three four-bar solos**. In each solo, include one trapdoor rest, one offbeat entrance, and one confident landing on a target note. Afterward, ask:
- Which surprise sounded coolest instead of just weird?
- Did my rescue bar actually restore the groove?
- Could I leave space without sounding timid?

Great phrasing is not about escaping mistakes forever. It is about making risky timing choices sound like the plan all along. 🎸
