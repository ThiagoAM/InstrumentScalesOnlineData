---
name: Piano Scale Balance Beam
description: Train both hands to stay balanced through dynamic shifts, accents, and thumb crossings inside one scale routine.
---
Choose **D major** or **Bb major**, two octaves if comfortable, and set the metronome to **72-88 BPM**. Imagine your scale is a balance beam: every note is easy when you are centered, but tiny tension, uneven tone, or a late thumb crossing can wobble the whole routine. Today you are practicing calm control, not heroic speed.
<!-- block -->
Round 1 is the **steady beam round**. Play the scale up and down with both hands together at one dynamic level. Listen for three things: equal tone, relaxed wrists, and thumb crossings that feel prepared instead of rushed. If one hand sounds bossier, lower its volume on purpose.
<!-- block -->
Round 2 is the **tilt round**. Keep the notes the same, but let the right hand grow slightly louder on the ascent and the left hand grow slightly louder on the descent. Nothing should collapse. The challenge is balancing intention without turning either hand into a bully.
<!-- block -->
Round 3 is the **beam markers round**. Add accents every **four notes** for one pass, then every **three notes** for the next. Keep the fingers close to the keys and prepare the thumb before each crossing. If the accents disturb the legato line, the beam is shaking.
<!-- block -->
Final challenge: perform **three complete runs** of the same scale.
1. even dynamic
2. shifting hand balance
3. moving accents

After the third run, ask:
- Which change hurt my hand balance the most?
- Did the thumb crossings stay early and quiet?
- Could I control the sound without tightening my shoulders or wrists?

Real piano technique is not just crossing the beam. It is learning how to stay graceful while the beam keeps asking harder questions. 🎹
